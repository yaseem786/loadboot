WHEREAS Dispatcher provides truck dispatch and back-office services; WHEREAS Carrier operates under its own USDOT/MC authority; NOW, THEREFORE the parties agree:
Article 1 — Definitions
1.1 “Dispatcher” means LoadBoot. “Carrier” means the motor carrier named in the signature block. “Load” means a shipment booked through Dispatcher. “Rate Confirmation” means the broker/shipper document setting the agreed rate for a Load.
1.2 “Booked through Dispatcher” means any Load for which Dispatcher sourced, presented, negotiated, or issued/received the Rate Confirmation on Carrier’s behalf, as recorded in the LoadBoot platform.
1.3 “Gross Line-Haul Revenue” means the base transportation charge stated on the Rate Confirmation. It EXCLUDES fuel-surcharge pass-through, lumper, detention, layover, TONU, and accessorials, unless a Rate Confirmation expressly bundles them into the line-haul.
1.4 “Dispatch Fee” is defined in Article 6 and Exhibit A. “Effective Date” is the date of Carrier’s electronic acceptance below.
Article 2 — Services
2.1 Dispatcher will use commercial best efforts to source freight, present load options, negotiate rates, and communicate with brokers and shippers on Carrier’s behalf, matched to the preferences Carrier provides.
2.2 Dispatcher will request and relay Rate Confirmations and related paperwork and provide back-office support (document routing, check-call coordination).
2.3 Dispatcher does not guarantee any number, frequency, lane, or rate of Loads. Carrier is not obligated to accept any Load presented.
Article 3 — Relationship of the Parties
3.1 Dispatcher is an independent contractor. Nothing herein creates employment, partnership, or joint venture.
3.2 Carrier retains sole and exclusive control over its drivers, equipment, routing, and manner and means of transportation.
3.3 Dispatcher is NOT a licensed property broker or freight forwarder, does not take possession of or title to freight, and is not liable for cargo loss, damage, delay, or freight claims.
Article 4 — Limited Authorization & Carrier Consent
4.1 Carrier grants Dispatcher a limited authorization to communicate with brokers/shippers, request and receive Rate Confirmations, and book Loads on Carrier’s behalf strictly per Carrier’s stated preferences.
4.2 Each booking is either (a) confirmed by Carrier before dispatch, or (b) auto-booked within the limits Carrier pre-approved; in both cases Carrier’s consent is recorded in the LoadBoot platform. Carrier may revoke or override any booking before dispatch.
Article 5 — Carrier Obligations
5.1 Carrier shall maintain active USDOT/MC authority and FMCSA good standing at all times.
5.2 Carrier shall maintain insurance of not less than $1,000,000 commercial auto liability and $100,000 motor-truck cargo, and provide certificates as brokers require.
5.3 Carrier shall use only qualified, licensed, DOT-compliant drivers and comply with all FMCSA/DOT rules (hours-of-service, ELD, drug & alcohol).
5.4 Carrier shall keep its authority, insurance, and equipment information accurate and promptly notify Dispatcher of any change, lapse, or suspension.
Article 6 — Dispatch Fee, Calculation & Payment
6.1 Carrier shall pay Dispatcher a Dispatch Fee of five percent (5%) of the Gross Line-Haul Revenue of each Load booked through Dispatcher and delivered.
6.2 WORKED EXAMPLE: a Load with $2,000.00 line-haul yields a Dispatch Fee of $100.00 (5%). Fuel surcharge, lumper, and detention are excluded.
6.3 The Fee is EARNED when the Load is booked through Dispatcher and delivered, and is collected by deduction from Carrier’s settlement or by invoice, per Carrier’s onboarding selection (including via a factoring Notice of Assignment where applicable).
6.4 Dispatcher provides an itemized settlement statement each pay period listing every Load, its Rate Confirmation number, Gross Line-Haul Revenue, and the Fee. Carrier may audit these records.
6.5 FEE DISPUTES: Carrier may dispute a specific Fee within fifteen (15) days of the statement by written notice identifying the Load. The Rate Confirmation and the LoadBoot record control. Undisputed Fees remain due.
6.6 NO FEE is charged on Loads Carrier sources independently and does not book through Dispatcher; Carrier is not charged twice for the same Load.
6.7 LATE PAYMENT: Fees unpaid 15 days after the statement may accrue interest at 1.5% per month (or the maximum allowed by law); Dispatcher may suspend Services until cured.
Article 7 — Cancellations, TONU & Detention
7.1 Cancelled Loads, TONU, detention, and layover are handled under Dispatcher’s then-current published policies.
7.2 No Fee applies to a cancelled Load unless a TONU or cancellation payment is received, in which case the 5% Fee applies to amounts actually paid.
Article 8 — Records & System of Record
8.1 The LoadBoot platform is the authoritative system of record for Loads, Rate Confirmations, bookings, Carrier consents, communications, and Fees.
8.2 In any dispute over whether a Load was booked through Dispatcher or over a Fee amount, the LoadBoot records and the applicable Rate Confirmation govern, absent clear evidence of error.
Article 9 — Term & Termination
9.1 Effective on the Effective Date; continues until terminated.
9.2 Either party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days’ written notice, or immediately for material breach, loss of authority, or lapse of insurance.
9.3 Fees earned before termination survive and remain payable.
Article 10 — Non-Exclusivity & Non-Circumvention
10.1 Non-exclusive unless a separate exclusivity addendum is signed; Carrier may use other dispatch services.
10.2 For the Term and 180 days after, Carrier shall not circumvent Dispatcher to avoid Fees on broker/shipper relationships first introduced to Carrier by Dispatcher, as shown in the LoadBoot records.
Article 11 — Confidentiality, Indemnification & Liability
11.1 Each party keeps the other’s non-public information confidential.
11.2 Carrier shall defend, indemnify, and hold Dispatcher harmless from claims arising out of Carrier’s operations, cargo, employees, or regulatory non-compliance.
11.3 Dispatcher’s aggregate liability shall not exceed the Fees paid in the three (3) months before the claim. Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential damages.
Article 12 — Governing Law & Dispute Resolution
12.1 Governed by the laws of the State of Texas.
12.2 Disputes resolved by binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Rules, seated in Dallas County, Texas; either party may seek injunctive relief in court.
Article 13 — Miscellaneous
13.1 This Agreement with Exhibit A and the onboarding terms is the entire agreement; amendments must be a signed writing (including electronic).
13.2 Severability and force majeure apply. Carrier may not assign without Dispatcher’s consent. Notices may be given electronically to the email on file.
13.3 The parties consent to electronic signatures and records (ESIGN Act / UETA). May be executed in counterparts.
