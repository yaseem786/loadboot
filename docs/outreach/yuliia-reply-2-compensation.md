# Reply to Yuliia — the compensation questions

## Read this first

She asked three things. Two are easy. The third — **"what are average monthly earnings for someone new in this role"** — is the one that can destroy you.

There is no average. Nobody has ever done this role at LoadBoot. She is the first.

If you invent a number, one of two things happens: she earns less and concludes you lied (and she is a European graduate with a LinkedIn and an inbox full of recruiters — she will say so), or she earns more and you gave away margin for nothing. Inventing a number is the single fastest way to look exactly like the two-bit dispatch shop you don't want to be.

**Saying "you'd be the first, so I won't invent a number — here is the real math instead" is what a serious software company sounds like.** Fake averages are what a scam sounds like. Your instinct to protect the brand is right; honesty *is* the way you protect it here, not confidence.

**One more thing — the name.** She's calling you Roman. That's fine, keep using it. But her contract and her Payoneer payments will carry your legal name. Mention it once, casually, in this message. If she discovers it on a payment three months from now, it looks like something you hid.
