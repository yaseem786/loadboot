## The email

**Subject:** TOP KNOTCH — documents received, five questions before we start dispatching

Hi Tarik,

Your documents came through — operating authority, insurance certificate, W-9 and the Bluevine bank letter are all on file and in review. Your Engaged Financial NOA is on file as well, so broker payments will route to your factor correctly from the first load.

One item is still outstanding: the **Dispatch Service Agreement**. You can sign it inside the portal under Documents — it takes about a minute.

Before I switch your account on, I'd rather ask you five questions than start guessing at your freight. Dispatching a box truck fleet badly is worse than not dispatching it at all, and I'd like to get this right from the first load.

**1. How many of the five trucks are actually moving right now, and how many do you want covered?**
If some are sitting, those are the ones I'd start with.

**2. Where does your freight come from today — another dispatcher, your own board access, or direct moving and relocation contracts?**
I'm not trying to replace something that already works for you. I want to know what gap I'm filling.

**3. What are the specs on the trucks?**
Box length, and whether they have a liftgate, pallet jack, ramp, or dock-height rear. On box truck freight this decides which loads you can even be offered, so I want it exact rather than assumed.

**4. What lanes do you run, and do the trucks come home at night?**
How far out from Glenolden you're willing to go — and whether that changes if the rate is right.

**5. What's your minimum acceptable rate, and are there brokers you're already set up with or won't work with?**
I'd rather know your floor now than waste your time bringing you loads you were always going to turn down.

**How I'd like to start.**

I'd suggest we begin with **one truck for two weeks**, not all five. If I keep that truck loaded and you're satisfied with the rates and the communication, we scale to the rest of the fleet. If I don't produce, there's no fee and I'll tell you straight that it isn't working rather than string you along.

I'll be honest with you about where we are: LoadBoot is a young company. What we've built is real — you've seen the portal, and you'll get live tracking, rate confirmations, document handling and settlement records on every load. What I'm not going to do is promise you volume I haven't delivered yet. One truck, two weeks, judge it on the results.

Answer the five above whenever suits you, or call me and we'll go through them in ten minutes.

## What to watch for in his answers

| Answer | What it means |
|---|---|
| "All five are running, I'm happy" | He's testing options. Low urgency — don't chase hard, keep it warm. |
| "Two or three are sitting" | **This is your opening.** Idle trucks cost him money every day. Move fast. |
| No liftgate / no pallet jack | Cuts him out of most freight-brokered box truck work. Be careful what you promise. |
| "I'll go anywhere for the right rate" | Easier to dispatch — you're not boxed into one region. |
| Refuses to name a floor rate | Warning sign. Means every load turns into an argument. Press politely for a number before you commit. |
| Won't sign the agreement | Do not dispatch. No agreement means no fee protection when the money lands. |

## Before you hit send

You need board access sorted before he answers — otherwise you'll have a willing carrier and nothing to offer him. Check current pricing yourself; anything I have on subscription costs may be out of date.
