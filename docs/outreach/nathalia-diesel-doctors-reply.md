**Subject:** Re: your questions — straight answers

Hi Nathalia,

Good questions — these are the ones carriers should ask and often don't. Straight answers below.

**1. How many trucks per dispatcher?**

Our cap is **[8–10 — set your real number]**, and I'd rather tell you why the number matters than just quote it. A dispatcher running 20+ trucks is a booking clerk: they take whatever covers the truck because they don't have time to negotiate. Below ten, they can actually work each load.

I'll also be straight with you about where we are: we are a young company and our books are not full. In practice that means more attention on your trucks, not less — but I'd rather you hear that from me than assume we're bigger than we are.

**2. Dedicated dispatcher?**

Yes. One person, who knows your equipment, your lanes and what you'll turn down. Not a rota, not a shared inbox. Everything is also visible to you in the portal, so if your dispatcher is off, the next person picks up your file with the full history rather than starting from scratch.

**3. How do we source loads?**

A combination, and honestly the mix depends on your equipment:

- **Load boards** — the bulk of it, worked against your own authority. You keep your MC and your customer relationships; we never take your freight.
- **Direct broker relationships** — built up on your MC as we run loads. These are earned over time, not something I'm going to claim we already have a wall of.
- **Our own marketplace** — this is the part that's different. LoadBoot has a broker and shipper portal where they post freight directly to our carriers. That inventory doesn't exist on a board because it comes to us first. It's growing rather than large, and I'd rather describe it accurately than oversell it.

**4. Lanes and regions?**

We run the lower 48 rather than claiming a niche we don't have. Rather than tell you what we specialise in, I'd rather ask what *you* run — where you're based, your usual lanes, how far out you'll go. If your lanes are somewhere we can't realistically keep you loaded, I'd sooner tell you that now than take you on and waste your time.

**5. What makes us different from dispatchers charging more?**

The honest answer is that **software does work that other shops pay people to do**, so we don't need to charge 8–10% to cover it.

Specifically, what you'd get that most dispatch shops don't provide:

- **Every rate in writing.** A rate confirmation on every load, before you roll. No verbal agreements that change at settlement.
- **Live GPS tracking** from your phone — no ELD hardware to buy, and it reads the ELD you already have if you use one. Your broker sees the truck moving, which is what gets you the next load.
- **Your own portal.** Loads, documents, rate confirmations, PODs, settlement records — all in one place, all yours. Most dispatchers run on WhatsApp and a spreadsheet, and when you leave you get nothing.
- **No ghost loads.** Every load on our board is source-verified. You don't waste calls on freight that was covered two days ago.
- **Flat 5% of linehaul, no contract, cancel any time.** Accessorials — detention, layover, TONU — are 100% yours; we don't take a cut of those.

You don't have to take my word for any of it. The platform is at loadboot.com and you can look through it before committing to anything.

**What I'd suggest:** tell me your equipment and your lanes, and let me show you what's realistically available for it. If the numbers don't work for you, no hard feelings — I'd rather lose the enquiry than the reputation.
