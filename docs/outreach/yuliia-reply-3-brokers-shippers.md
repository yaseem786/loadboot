# Follow-up to Yuliia — it isn't only carriers

Your own website already publishes this exact model. `build_site.py`, the Agent programme:

> "Bring clients in pairs (a carrier + a broker/shipper, or post loads yourself) and earn **1% of the gross on every GPS-verified delivered load** your chain touches — paid from LoadBoot's own fee."

**Fix it now, before you send anything else.** Put her on the published agent terms: **1% of gross on delivered loads**, plus the **$35 per verified carrier** on top (agents don't do document chasing — she will, so she earns more than a standard agent, not less). ## The second thing you must be precise about

**Brokers and shippers pay LoadBoot nothing.** They use the platform free — that's your published position.

So there is no money in a broker signing up. The money appears only when a load they post is **hauled by a LoadBoot carrier and delivered**. If you let her think "bring brokers, get paid", she'll bring ten brokers, see zero, and quit — and tell people you wasted her time.

Said correctly, though, this is the *better* half of the offer: one active broker posting freight every week generates far more delivered loads than one carrier does. That's a genuinely strong pitch. It just has to be stated as **paid on delivered loads**, not on signups.
