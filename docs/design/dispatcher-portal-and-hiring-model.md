## Salary model (monthly, on assignment)
- **No carrier assigned = no salary** (dispatcher sits in the pool). Salary **starts when CC assigns â‰Ą1 carrier**, paid **monthly**.
- Scale salary with the **book** (more assigned carriers/trucks = higher salary), so it tracks the work + the 5% revenue it generates. Option: **base + performance** (loaded %, on-time, rate quality, carrier retention).
- **Margin rule:** total dispatcher salary must stay **below the 5% revenue** from that dispatcher's carriers (thin early with 1â€“3 trucks, healthy as the book fills to ~8â€“15 trucks). One dispatcher on many trucks Ă— 5% >> one offshore monthly salary â†’ strong margin at scale.

## "Every carrier gets a dedicated dispatcher â€” guaranteed"
- Offer **guaranteed managed dispatch** to win carriers (a strong differentiator vs self-serve boards). Because **salary only starts on assignment**, you never pay idle dispatchers â€” **hire as carriers arrive** (~1 dispatcher per ~10â€“15 trucks).
- **Early margin is thin** (few trucks per dispatcher) â€” accept it as a **growth investment**; margin improves automatically as each dispatcher's book fills. Keep hiring paced to carrier inflow so no dispatcher is under-loaded for long.

## Compliance guardrails (unchanged, critical)
