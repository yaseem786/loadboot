# LoadBoot — Dispatcher Portal + Hiring/Verification Model (Agent → Dispatcher)

Owner vision: rename **Agent → Dispatcher**; one portal (same login/signup) that is **open for
referral** but requires **strict verification to become a paid dedicated dispatcher**; CC hires,
verifies, assigns a carrier, and instructs; salary paid **monthly, only when a carrier is assigned**;
**every carrier who wants one gets a dedicated dispatcher** (even at 5%, even if early margin is thin).
Deep-researched below. Get a transportation attorney to confirm the dispatcher agreement + the
multi-carrier "no-allocation" structure before scaling.

## One portal, TWO levels (same account, same login/signup)
1. **Referrer (open — light KYC):** anyone can sign up, get a referral link, bring carriers/brokers/shippers, earn **1% of gross on delivered loads their chain touches + downline overrides**. No dispatching. (This is today's agent program — keep it, just renamed.)
2. **Paid Dedicated Dispatcher (strict — hired by CC):** a referrer/user applies to become a paid dispatcher → **separate, strict verification + skills screening** → CC approves + assigns carrier(s) → **monthly salary** starts. Does the actual dispatching for assigned carriers.
- A person can be **both** (refers AND is a paid dispatcher). Salary + 1% referral can stack (referral only on their own referred chain).

## Becoming a paid dispatcher — what to collect + STRICT screening (before salary)
Collect (in the portal application) + CC verifies before hiring:
- **Identity + right to work as contractor** (govt ID, address, contact, payout method for salary).
- **Signed Dispatcher Contractor Agreement** (LoadBoot ↔ dispatcher) + **Dispatch Service Agreement/POA** template they will use with carriers (attorney text) + NDA/confidentiality + no-money-handling + no-re-brokering clauses.
- **Experience/qualifications** — HS/GED min; logistics/transport background preferred; prior dispatch / broker-clerk / driver / logistics / customer-service experience is a strong plus (less training). [Vault requirements](https://vault.com/professions/truck-dispatchers/requirements) · [Indeed how-to](https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/career-development/how-to-become-a-truck-dispatcher)
- **Skills tested (strictly, before hire):** clear **English communication** (US brokers/carriers), **rate negotiation** (mock broker call), **load-board proficiency** (DAT/Truckstop + LoadBoot board), **FMCSA/HOS + regulations knowledge**, **US geography/lanes**, organization, multitasking under pressure, TMS/GPS tech. [ZipRecruiter skills](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/e/Truck-Dispatch-What-are-the-key-skills-and-qualifications-needed-to-thrive-as-a-Truck-Dispatcher-and-why-are-they-important) · [Indeed JD](https://www.indeed.com/hire/job-description/truck-dispatcher)
- **Reference check + background check + short paid trial/probation** (evaluate on real carriers before confirming).
- Verification lives in the CC (a **Dispatcher Verification queue**, like the carrier/agent doc-review you already have).

## CC (Command Center) workflow
1. **Review application** → interview → skills test → **approve / reject** (Dispatcher Verification queue).
2. **Assign carrier(s)** to the approved dispatcher (carrier↔dispatcher link).
3. **Send SOP/instructions** — how to hunt loads for THIS carrier: target lanes, equipment, **minimum acceptable rate**, home-time, do's & don'ts, and the compliance rules (per-carrier only, never touch money, verify the broker).
4. **Dispatcher hunts loads** (LoadBoot board + the carrier's own DAT/Truckstop seat) **for that carrier** and forwards the best options.
5. **LoadBoot verifies the BROKER** behind each load (FMCSA authority + $75k bond + insurance + credit) and **presents the load to the carrier to accept** — the carrier books directly with that broker (rate con = broker → carrier). *"LoadBoot verifies and posts to the carrier" = surfacing a verified broker's load for the carrier to book — NOT LoadBoot taking/re-posting freight.*
6. **On delivery** → carrier billed **5%** → dispatcher's **monthly salary** paid → referral 1% (if any) accrues.

## Dispatcher responsibilities (job list)
[100Hires JD](https://100hires.com/truck-dispatcher-job-description.html) · [UpperInc](https://www.upperinc.com/blog/truck-dispatcher-job-description/) · [RouteOne duties](https://routeonedispatch.com/blog/truck-dispatcher-duties-and-responsibilities)
- **Find/hunt loads** for the assigned carrier (LoadBoot board + external load boards); keep the truck loaded, minimize deadhead.
- **Negotiate rates** with brokers; hit/beat the carrier's minimum rate.
- **Verify the broker** (authority/bond/insurance/credit via LoadBoot) BEFORE booking.
- **Book the load** (rate con broker→carrier), generate the dispatch pack, send pickup/delivery details to the driver.
- **Plan routes/lanes**, appointments, reloads; respect **HOS**.
- **Track the trip**, check calls, handle exceptions (delays, breakdowns, detention/lumper/TONU documentation).
- **Paperwork:** rate con, BOL, POD collection, accessorial docs; keep everything logged on LoadBoot.
- **Communicate** with driver + broker; resolve problems; keep the carrier informed.
- **Never** touch freight money · **never** re-broker/reassign a load · **never** post/offer freight to the open market · **only** dispatch for their assigned carrier(s).

## Salary model (monthly, on assignment)
- **No carrier assigned = no salary** (dispatcher sits in the pool). Salary **starts when CC assigns ≥1 carrier**, paid **monthly**.
- Scale salary with the **book** (more assigned carriers/trucks = higher salary), so it tracks the work + the 5% revenue it generates. Option: **base + performance** (loaded %, on-time, rate quality, carrier retention).
- **Margin rule:** total dispatcher salary must stay **below the 5% revenue** from that dispatcher's carriers (thin early with 1–3 trucks, healthy as the book fills to ~8–15 trucks). One dispatcher on many trucks × 5% >> one offshore monthly salary → strong margin at scale.

## "Every carrier gets a dedicated dispatcher — guaranteed"
- Offer **guaranteed managed dispatch** to win carriers (a strong differentiator vs self-serve boards). Because **salary only starts on assignment**, you never pay idle dispatchers — **hire as carriers arrive** (~1 dispatcher per ~10–15 trucks).
- **Early margin is thin** (few trucks per dispatcher) — accept it as a **growth investment**; margin improves automatically as each dispatcher's book fills. Keep hiring paced to carrier inflow so no dispatcher is under-loaded for long.

## Compliance guardrails (unchanged, critical)
- **Per-carrier sourcing only** — a dispatcher finds loads FOR one specific carrier's truck; **never "here's a load, which carrier?"** (that's allocating = broker). Each carrier under a written dispatch agreement. (Attorney to confirm multi-carrier structure.)
- **No freight-money custody** — broker pays carrier/factor directly; LoadBoot only bills its 5%.
- **Broker of record verified** on every load; the carrier is protected by that broker's $75k bond.
- **Load boards** via the **carrier's own account + dispatcher seat** (cleanest).

## Targets, capacity & salary (concrete — owner sets final figures)

### How many trucks a dispatcher manages (capacity)
- **Start 5–8 trucks**, ramp to **10–15** as they prove out; simple/repeat lanes can go **20–30** at scale. [ProfitableVenture](https://www.profitableventure.com/how-many-truck-per-dispatcher/)
- Tell each dispatcher their **current book + target book** (e.g., "you hold 6 trucks, target 10 in 60 days").

### Monthly performance targets (KPIs the dispatcher must hit) [FreightWaves KPIs](https://www.freightwaves.com/news/the-kpi-breakdown-every-dispatcher-should-know) · [Sage trucking KPIs](https://www.sage.com/en-us/blog/trucking-kpis/)
- **Weekly gross per truck:** keep each truck at target (OTR owner-ops gross **$4,000–8,000/week** — aim mid/high).
- **Truck utilization ≥ 85%** (best-in-class 80–85%).
- **Deadhead < 10–12%** of miles.
- **On-time delivery ≥ 98%**.
- **Loads/week per truck:** OTR **2–3** long-haul, regional **3–5**.
- **Book/quality:** ramp book to target, **low cancels**, **carrier retention** (renew month over month).
- These KPIs feed the dispatcher's **performance bonus** AND the carrier's health score.

### Salary — market-based (Pakistan/offshore dispatchers for US carriers)
Pakistan market for US-trucking dispatchers (2026): beginners **PKR 25k–40k/mo**; experienced **PKR 60k+**; US-campaign packages **PKR 60k–75k + commission**, some **PKR 100k base + commission**; remote-for-US-clients effective **$800–$3,000+/mo** depending on trucks + commission. [Glassdoor Lahore](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/lahore-truck-dispatcher-salary-SRCH_IL.0,6_IM1625_KO7,23.htm) · [IDT Pakistan earnings](https://idtpakistan.pk/blog/truck-dispatcher-salary-2026-earnings-guide/)

**Recommended structure (base + per-truck + performance) — starts only on carrier assignment:**
- **Base:** modest fixed (e.g., **PKR 50k–80k/mo** ≈ $180–290, by experience) — begins when CC assigns the first carrier.
- **Per-truck increment:** small amount **per active assigned truck** (tracks the 5% revenue each truck brings). More trucks in the book → more pay.
- **Performance bonus:** tied to the KPIs above (utilization, on-time, gross/truck, retention) — motivates keeping trucks EARNING, which also grows LoadBoot's 5%.
- **Effective total:** ~**PKR 60k–150k+ ($220–540+)/mo** for a productive dispatcher with a full book; more for top performers with big books.

**The margin rule (must hold):** total dispatcher pay (base + per-truck + bonus) stays **below the 5% revenue** from that dispatcher's carriers.
- *Illustration:* dispatcher holds **8 trucks**, each grossing ~**$25k/mo** → $200k/mo gross → **LoadBoot 5% = $10,000/mo**; dispatcher pay ~**$400–700/mo** → LoadBoot keeps **~$9,300+/mo** from that pod. Strong margin at a full book.
- **Early (1–3 trucks):** base > the thin 5%, so margin is **negative-to-thin at first** — accepted growth cost; it flips positive as the book fills. Pace hiring to carrier inflow so dispatchers reach a full book quickly.

### What CC tells each dispatcher (monthly)
- Your **book** (assigned carriers/trucks) + **target book**.
- Your **KPI targets** (gross/truck, utilization, deadhead, on-time, cancels).
- Per-carrier **SOP** (lanes, minimum rate, home-time, do's/don'ts, compliance rules).
- Your **salary = base + per-truck + performance bonus**; how the bonus is earned.
