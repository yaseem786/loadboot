# LoadBoot — INDUSTRY COMPLIANCE SOP (owner directive 2026-07-02, verbatim structure)
_The owner's full US-trucking document/compliance specification. This file is the CANON for tasks D1–D8.
Status tags: [LEGAL] = federal/state law · [REQUIRED] = operationally required · [CONDITIONAL] = commodity/
payment/situation-specific · [OPTIONAL] = best practice._

## The 3 document levels
1. Onboarding / Master Agreements (relationship start — signed ONCE)
2. Per-load documents (every load: tender, rate confirmation, dispatch sheet, BOL)
3. Delivery / Payment documents (POD, receipts, invoice packet; broker records 3 years)

## §1 Shipper↔Broker
A. Shipper needs from broker (onboarding): legal name/address/contact [REQ]; active FMCSA broker authority
/ MC [LEGAL]; USDOT/MC verification [REQ]; $75,000 BMC-84 bond or BMC-85 trust [LEGAL — from 2026-01-16,
security below $75k not restored in 7 days can suspend authority]; broker W-9 [REQ]; COI (GL, E&O,
contingent cargo, cyber if demanded) [CONTRACTUAL]; Broker-Shipper Agreement [REQ]; bank/payment
instructions [REQ]; references [OPT]; claims procedure [REQ]; tracking capability [OPT/enterprise].
B. Broker-Shipper Agreement — 30 clauses (parties' legal names, authority representation, scope, tender &
acceptance procedure, rates/payment terms, fuel surcharge method, detention/layover/TONU/lumper rules,
claims procedure + filing deadline, cargo value limits, insurance requirements, indemnification, carrier
selection standard, NO unauthorized re-brokering, confidentiality, non-solicitation, tracking requirements,
temp-controlled / hazmat / food-grade requirements, records retention, dispute resolution, governing law,
termination, force majeure, fraudulent payment-change verification, accessorial approval procedure,
subcontractor/co-broker permission, BOL-vs-master conflict control, shipper accurate-cargo duty).
MASTER = signed once at onboarding; per-load tender rides under it.

## §2–3 Broker needs from shipper + due diligence
Onboarding: legal name, DBA, addresses, AP contact, ops contact, credit application [REQ]; EIN verify /
shipper W-9 [OPT]; signed agreement, payment terms (Net 15/30/45), billing instructions, PO/ref format,
cargo profile, cargo insurance requirements, claims contact, facility rules + appointment process,
detention/layover approval process [REQ]; sample BOL, lanes/volume [OPT]; hazmat/food/high-value [COND].
Due diligence: identity verification (entity real, address match, domain email, AP verified, signer
authorized, bank in company name) [REQ/BP]; credit check (report, days-to-pay, references, disputes,
lawsuits, limit, prepay for first loads) [OPT legally / strongly required]; commodity check (exact
commodity, legality, declared value, new/used/salvage, theft-sensitive, temp, food-grade, hazmat class +
UN/NA, packaging) [REQ — hazmat shipping papers are the shipper/offeror's LEGAL duty; carrier may not
accept hazmat without them]; dimensions & weight (total, pallets, dims, stackable, over-dim, axle, equipment,
liftgate/tarp/chains) [REQ]; pickup/delivery feasibility (addresses, appt/FCFS, hours, parking, driver
assist, dock type, residential/jobsite/military, CDL/route restrictions, stops, unload, team) [REQ];
financial terms (linehaul, FSC, detention hours+rate, layover, TONU, lumper, extra stops, payment docs)
[REQ]; cargo insurance fit (value within carrier limit, commodity exclusions — electronics/seafood/
alcohol/tobacco/copper/pharma/reefer, breakdown coverage, high-value endorsement) [REQ — federal cargo
filing normally not mandatory; $750k public-liability minimum for ≥10,001 lb for-hire, contracts may demand
more]; special commodity compliance (food sanitary-transport duties, hazmat placards/permits, alcohol state
permits, pharma, oversize permits/escorts, government clearance).

## §5 Broker↔Dispatcher legal rule
Broker's transportation relationship is with the CARRIER; dispatcher = carrier's authorized agent. Without
broker authority a dispatcher must NOT: solicit shippers directly, take payment from broker/factoring,
accept loads without a truck, transfer loads between carriers, allocate across overlapping carriers at own
discretion, or stand as independent party in the shipping contract. FMCSA: written agency contract with the
carrier, work at carrier's direction, paid only by the carrier.

## §6–7 Carrier packet + per-load dispatcher→broker
Carrier packet: legal name, MC, USDOT [REQ]; active property authority [LEGAL]; signed Broker-Carrier
Agreement, W-9, COI direct from agent [REQ]; auto liability [LEGAL/CONTRACTUAL]; cargo insurance
[CONTRACTUAL]; reefer breakdown [COND]; GL [CONTRACTUAL]; NOA / voided-check-ACH / factoring info [COND];
SAFER check (status, authority, inspections, crashes, OOS, rating — never dispatch Out-of-Service;
Conditional = enhanced review) [REQ]; equipment info [REQ]; driver name+phone per load [REQ]; tracking
consent [CONTRACTUAL]; hazmat cert [COND]; dispatcher agency authorization/limited POA [COND]; emergency
contact + payment email [REQ].
Per load dispatcher gives broker: carrier legal name, MC/DOT, truck/trailer type + numbers (before pickup),
empty location+time, driver name+phone, HOS confirmation [REQ operationally], ETA, negotiated rate,
tracking acceptance, RC email, driver-understands confirmation, SIGNED Rate Confirmation [REQ].
Payment: broker pays carrier/factoring, NEVER the dispatcher; dispatcher's fee comes from carrier.

## §8 Broker→carrier per load — D4
Before verbal acceptance [REQ]: broker legal name+MC, load ID, origin/destination, deadhead estimate,
pickup/delivery dates, commodity, weight, equipment, stops, loaded miles, offered rate, tracking, driver
assist/no-touch, hazmat, temp, special requirements, appt/FCFS.
After acceptance — RATE CONFIRMATION [REQ]: both correct legal names/MCs, load #, total carrier payment,
pickup/delivery details, commodity+weight, equipment, appointment times, detention policy, layover policy,
TONU policy, lumper reimbursement, tracking penalties, late-delivery penalties, POD deadline, invoice
instructions, special instructions, signature/date. Dispatcher must never modify an RC.

## §9–10 Dispatcher-Carrier onboarding + agreement — D2/D3
Packet (30 items incl. service agreement [REQ], limited POA where signing [REQ], authority verify [LEGAL],
W-9, COI, factoring/NOA [COND], truck/trailer/equipment, driver roster, HOS process, preferred lanes,
avoid-areas [OPT], min RPM, max deadhead, home time, commodity restrictions, hazmat capability [COND],
touch-freight preference, tolls [OPT], emergency contact, fee + payment authorization, dedicated email,
upload method, after-hours rules, final-acceptance authority). NEVER take carrier email passwords.
Agreement (30 clauses): agent-not-broker, under carrier authority, no shipper solicitation, no reassignment,
carrier final accept/reject, fee %/fixed + base + accessorial treatment + frequency, POA scope, negotiation
+ RC-signing + packet authority, liability limits, insurance/driver-safety/HOS = carrier responsibility,
cargo claims, confidentiality, data protection, NO double brokering, NO forced dispatch, termination, unpaid
fees, disputes, governing law, records ownership, communication rules, no broker/factoring payment to
dispatcher, auto-stop on authority/insurance suspension, no bank/factoring change without written approval.

## §11 DISPATCH SHEET (dispatcher→carrier/driver, every booked load, 30 items) — D5
broker name+MC+contact, load #, agreed rate, loaded/deadhead miles, all-in RPM, pickup address/date/time/
ref, delivery address/date/time, receiver contact, commodity, weight, pieces, equipment, temp, seal,
appt/FCFS, tracking instructions, detention requirements, lumper process, special instructions, emergency
contact, RC copy, route/toll estimate, fuel/parking notes [when useful], documents to collect, POD
submission instructions. Plus the dispatcher service list (load search → invoice packet → next-load planning).

## §12 PRE-BOOKING FINAL CHECKS (all [REQ]) — D6
1 Truck (empty, location, time, trailer, mechanical, clean/odor-free, reefer, fuel) · 2 Driver (willing,
verified, reachable legally, home-time, team, CDL/hazmat) · 3 HOS (11-hr driving / 14-hr window after 10 off
— estimate only, LEGAL duty stays with driver+carrier) · 4 Broker verification (authority+bond active,
phone/email match, RC from legal entity, credit history, factoring approves, no fraud flags, no suspicious
bank change — FMCSA warns on unauthorized brokerage / identity fraud) · 5 Load details · 6 Financial
analysis (gross, loaded+all-in RPM, deadhead, fuel, tolls, fees, lumper, NET, next-load market) · 7 RC
review (correct name/amount/times/penalties, no hidden deductions) · 8 WRITTEN carrier approval before "book it".

## §13–14 Pickup + delivery docs — D8
BOL [interstate for-hire: carrier must issue receipt/BOL]: consignor, consignee, origin, destination,
package count, description, weight — driver checks carrier name, counts, seal, temp, damage notation, both
signatures, date/time. Dispatcher never alters a BOL.
After delivery: signed POD/BOL [REQ], receiver sign/name/date/time [REQ], lumper receipt / detention
in-out / scale / washout / temp records [COND], photos [OPT/BP], carrier invoice + RC packet [REQ].
Broker transaction records (shipper, carrier, BOL #, compensation, carrier payment) retained 3 YEARS;
transaction parties have review right.

## §15 STOP / REJECT — never book when — D7
broker authority inactive · carrier authority inactive/OOS · insurance expired · broker name/MC/email/phone
mismatch · insufficient HOS · cargo value > insurance limit · concealed commodity · wrong carrier name on
RC · broker offers dispatcher direct payment · accepted without available truck · asked to use another
carrier's name on BOL · asked to move on another MC · unverified bank/factoring change · missing hazmat
papers · unclear reefer temp · legally impossible schedule · weight > capacity · double-brokering doubt ·
refusal to put verbal terms in writing.

## Final flow
Shipper→Broker: Master Agreement + Credit + Load Tender · Broker→Carrier (via dispatcher): Carrier Packet +
Broker-Carrier Agreement + Rate Confirmation · Carrier→Dispatcher: Dispatch Agreement + POA + authority/
insurance + truck/driver/HOS · Dispatcher→Driver: verified load + rate analysis + RC + Dispatch Sheet ·
Shipper@pickup→Driver: BOL + cargo + special docs · Carrier after delivery→Broker: POD + invoice + RC +
approved receipts.
