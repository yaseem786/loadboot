## 4. TIER 4 — Community answers (Reddit / Quora / TruckersReport)
(Value first; link sirf jab thread directly puchhe. Apne flair/bio mein loadboot.com rakho.)

**Q: "How do I actually get paid detention?"**
> Three things get detention paid: (1) detention terms IN the rate con before you haul — not negotiated after; (2) proof of arrival time — a GPS stamp or geotagged photo the minute you hit the gate, plus one when you leave; (3) invoice it same-day with the stamps attached. Standard is 2 hours free then $40–75/hr. If the broker didn't put detention in writing, you're asking for a favor, not a payment. I wrote a full policy breakdown here if useful: loadboot.com/detention-pay-policy.html

**Q: "Are load boards full of fake/ghost loads?"**
> Yes, and it's often deliberate. A posted load that booked 3 hours ago still generates calls — and every call is a carrier the broker can pitch other freight. That's why booking one load averages 15–20 calls / 3–6 hours. Protect yourself: call the newest posts first, ask "is this still available and what's the pickup number" in the first 20 seconds, and drop boards that repost the same load daily. Longer breakdown: loadboot.com/ghost-loads-load-board-problems.html

**Q: "What is a TONU and what should I charge?"**
> TONU = Truck Ordered, Not Used — you showed up (or headed out) and the load cancelled. Standard is a $250 flat fee, range $150–$350, more if you deadheaded far. Two rules: TONU must be in the rate con BEFORE pickup, and you need proof you actually dispatched (GPS or timestamped photo). Rate con guide: loadboot.com/how-to-read-a-rate-confirmation.html

**Q: "Are dispatch services worth it / what do they cost?"**
> Typical dispatch fees run 3–10% of gross. The math: if a dispatcher lifts your average rate 10–15% and saves you 3+ hours/day of load hunting, 5% pays for itself. Red flags: forced dispatch, long contracts, flat monthly fees regardless of loads, and anyone who won't put their fee in writing. Full cost breakdown with the math: loadboot.com/how-much-does-a-truck-dispatcher-cost.html

**Q: "Per diem — do I need meal receipts?"**
> No receipts needed for the meals themselves — that's the point of per diem. 2026: $80/day, 80% deductible = $64/night. What you DO need is proof of the NIGHTS away (logs, ELD data, trip records). That proof gap is why most owner-operators underclaim. W-2 company drivers can't claim it at all post-TCJA — owner-ops on Schedule C only. Full guide: loadboot.com/truck-driver-per-diem-2026.html
